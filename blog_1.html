TECHNICAL BLOG - PADDING, BORDER AND MARGIN

In this short technical blog, I'm going to explain the CSS concepts of PADDING, BORDER and MARGIN using a comparison most of us can easily understand â€“ hanging paintings on a wall. I'll also be showing what the basic code looks like to manipulate these 3 properties.

So here you are! Begin the metaphor!

You have a blank wall. That is your WEBPAGE.

You have paintings you want to put on the wall. That is your CONTENT. CONTENT on a WEBPAGE is the words, the ideas, the pictures, the videos the things that someone would come to a website wanting to ingest.

Also, you are a master frame maker and you can make frames of any thickness to hang your paintings in. This is your BORDER.

Let's start by seeing how we can change the properties of our BORDER, then we can explain PADDING and MARGIN.

Three common properties for a BORDER are Width, Colour and Style. Here's two BORDERs, one with a 5 pixel wide, green, solid BORDER, the other one with a doubly wide (10px), red, dotted BORDER.

Next, let's learn about PADDING. This is the distance between your CONTENT (the painting) and the BORDER. Here's 2 sizes of PADDING, the first is 15 pixels in size, the second is 30. (Note that the CONTENT is aligned to the left by default.)

Finally, the MARGIN denotes the amount of empty space outside the BORDER. This is the distance you choose to hang your paintings apart. You can think of the MARGIN as the amount of personal space each painting has. In this example the MARGIN properties of the top box are being changed. The first is 20 pixels in size, the second is 40. (Note that increasing the MARGIN distance effects the distance from the edge of the page, as well as other CONTENT on the page.)

Did this make sense? Are you a master painting hanger now? Are you the benevolent god of your metaphorical wall?
